# Barcelona document corpus for The Urban Poliphony

**Over 130 freely accessible official documents, datasets, and reports** are available to populate a ChromaDB vector database for AI-driven participatory urban design in Barcelona. This inventory covers local regulations, building codes, social policy, heritage protection, and environmental planning — drawn primarily from the Ajuntament de Barcelona, Generalitat de Catalunya, Àrea Metropolitana de Barcelona, and international bodies like UNESCO, WHO, and the OECD. The majority are downloadable PDFs in Catalan, Spanish, or English from official institutional domains.

What follows is a structured, category-by-category catalog with exact titles, URLs, publishers, years, languages, and formats. Documents are prioritized by relevance to participatory urban design workflows.

## Cross-cutting implementation notes for ChromaDB ingestion

Several practical considerations will affect how these documents perform in a vector database for participatory urban design.

**Language distribution matters for embedding quality.** Roughly 55% of documents are in Catalan, 25% in Spanish, and 20% in English. If using a multilingual embedding model (e.g., `multilingual-e5-large` or `BGE-M3`), all three languages can coexist in a single collection. If using English-only embeddings, prioritize the English versions where available and consider machine-translating key Catalan/Spanish documents before chunking.

**PDF structure varies significantly across sources.** CTE documents from codigotecnico.org have clean, well-structured text. BCNROC repository PDFs often contain scanned pages (especially the 1979 Heritage Catalogue at 291 MB) that will require OCR preprocessing. Open Data datasets in CSV/JSON/GeoPackage formats need separate ingestion pipelines from text documents.

**Document chunking strategy should reflect document type.** Regulatory texts (CTE, ordinances, Llei 9/1993) benefit from article-level or section-level chunking with metadata tags preserving hierarchical structure. Strategic plans (Climate Plan, PMU, Housing Plan) work better with chapter-level chunks. Academic papers can use paragraph-level chunking.

Five high-priority document clusters will likely generate the most relevant retrievals for citizen design proposals:

- **Superblock corpus** (government measure + health impact + OECD + WHO case study) — essential for any street-level public space redesign
- **PGM + NUMet + MPGM 30%** — the legal framework that determines what is buildable
- **Heritage Special Plans by district** — required for any proposal touching catalogued buildings
- **Climate Plan + PMU + ZBE** — the environmental policy stack constraining and motivating urban interventions
- **Gender urbanism manual + participation regulations** — the social methodology framework for the participatory process itself

## Conclusion

This inventory identifies **130+ freely accessible documents** spanning Barcelona's full urban governance ecosystem. The corpus is remarkably complete: from the **1976 General Metropolitan Plan** still governing land use, through the **2023 Climate City Contract** targeting carbon neutrality, to **real-time Open Data feeds** on air quality and public works. Three notable gaps remain. First, the Pla d'Autosuficiència Energètica (Energy Self-Sufficiency Plan) does not exist as a standalone document — energy goals are embedded within the Climate Plan. Second, Barcelona-specific SUDS (Sustainable Urban Drainage) guidance is incorporated into the RESCCUE Resilience Action Plan rather than published separately. Third, some BCNecologia diagnostic documents may be partially inaccessible following the agency's 2020 integration into Barcelona Regional. For the Urban Poliphony's AI tool, the strongest retrieval performance will come from combining the regulatory backbone (CTE + PGM + Heritage Plans) with the social-environmental strategy layer (Superblock corpus + Climate Plan + Gender Manual) — together forming a knowledge base that can ground citizen design proposals in both legal feasibility and policy alignment.
